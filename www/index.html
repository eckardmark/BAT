Baseline Assessment

The Baseline Assessment Tool for iPad establishes valid starting points from which to track students’ academic and personal growth. This provides the first step in demonstrating your school’s added value over other educational options as your students move up in grades. Given to students in kindergarten through 2nd grade, it also detects mild learning and attention difficulties early on and provides an accurate description of a child’s learning characteristics.

The constructs and measuring instruments used in the baseline assessment are based on a thorough examination of peer-reviewed journal articles. The BAT collects data in the following areas:

  • Early reading ability (phonological awareness, letter-word recognition, comprehension, and fluency)
  • Early math ability (counting, number recognition, number sense)
  • Executive function (sustained attention, inhibitory control)
  • Visual-Motor abilities (copying geometric figures, fine motor control)
  • Strengths and weaknesses in visual, auditory and verbal processing to assess learning style
  • Temperament (shyness, activity level, sociability)
  • Emotional functioning (frustration tolerance, ability to delay gratification, test anxiety, self esteem)
  • Moral character (empathy, moral identity, altruism)
